✂️ CRISPR-Cas9 Lab

Design guide RNAs, simulate Cas9 cutting, predict off-targets, and explore therapeutic applications — all interactively.

🧬

Guide RNA (gRNA)

A 20-nucleotide sequence that directs Cas9 to the exact genomic location. Paired with PAM sequence (NGG for SpCas9).

✂️

Cas9 Nuclease

The molecular scissors. Two nuclease domains (RuvC and HNH) each cut one strand of DNA, creating a double-strand break.

🔧

DNA Repair

The cell repairs the break via NHEJ (error-prone, creates knockouts) or HDR (precise editing with a template).

💊

Therapeutic Use

From sickle cell disease (Casgevy, 2023) to cancer immunotherapy (CAR-T enhancement) — CRISPR is transforming medicine.

The CRISPR-Cas9 Mechanism

1

Design a 20-nt guide RNA (gRNA) complementary to your target DNA sequence

2

The gRNA + Cas9 complex scans the genome for the PAM sequence (5'-NGG-3')

3

Cas9 unwinds the DNA and checks for gRNA complementarity — must match 20/20 bases

4

On match: RuvC cuts the non-template strand, HNH cuts the template strand → DSB

5

Cell repairs via NHEJ (indels → knockout) or HDR with template (precise edit)

🏆 Nobel Prize 2020: Jennifer Doudna and Emmanuelle Charpentier won the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for developing CRISPR-Cas9 as a genome editing tool. In 2023, Casgevy became the first approved CRISPR therapy — curing sickle cell disease.
